What is digital accessibility?

Process of removing and preventing barriers to access so everyone can enjoy your work equally.

Physical

Muscle control limitations, joint disorders, missing limbs

Auditory

Partial or complete loss of hearing in one or both ears

Visual

Partial vision loss ("low vision"), or complete loss ("blindness"). Also includes people with reduced or increased sensitivity to certain colors ("color blindness").

Cognitive

Cognitive, learning, and neurological disorders including neurodiversity, neurological, behavioral, and mental health disorders.

Temporary

Broken limbs, vertigo or fatigue after medical procedures, recovery from accidents, undergoing physical theraphy.

Situational

High-stress environments like disaster/accident scenarios, distraction, broken trackpad/mouse forcing keyboard use.

For businesses

  • Reduce risk of legal action (demand letters, lawsuits, etc)
  • Considering accessibility throughout the SDLC is far easier and cheaper than trying to hack and layer it in during a crisis.
  • Reach a larger market share, potentially cornering a market segment that has been underserved.
  • Improve company image by engaging in objectively positive, socially responsible activity.

Spend money on talent, not lawyers!

For developers

  • Because it relies heavily on using native tech as intended, it naturally encourages cleaner code that is easier to maintain.
  • Sought after skill in modern market, and is increasing in value right now
  • Personal and professional growth. Learning new things is important.

WCAG to the rescue!

12 guidelines based on 4 principles

  • Perceivable - everyone can access the content
  • Operable - everyone can interact with it
  • Understandable - UI and content is clear
  • Robust - works with many user agents + AT
